Cheat on your taxes

February 4 by Steve

When it comes to getting an early start on retirement savings, an E*TRADE IRA for Minors represents an opportunity. Opened by the minor’s parent or legal guardian, it’s an IRA opened for a child under 18 who has earned income. You’re never too young to start saving for retirement. An E*TRADE IRA for Minors helps make it a whole lot easier.

Why a Roth IRA for Minors?

  • No annual IRA fees1 and no account minimums
  • The same potential tax advantages as either a Traditional or Roth IRA depending on which account type is opened
  • Contribute up to $5,500 or 100% of compensation per year, whichever is less
  • Flexibility to use the funds to pay for college expenses
  • Same features as other Traditional or Roth IRAs
  • Ability to start tax advantaged savings early for minors with earned income

Eligibility Requirements

  • Eligible participants are under the age of 18
  • The minor must have earned income for the tax year in which a contribution is made
  • The minor must be a U.S. citizen or resident with a valid U.S. address. If the minor is not a U.S. citizen or resident, works for a broker-dealer, or is a director or significant shareholder of a publicly traded company, additional requirements apply
  • A custodian (parent or legal guardian) establishes, trades, and maintains account for the benefit of the minor. Only one custodian is allowed per account
  • IRAs for Minors (Traditional & Roth) must be established by the tax filing deadline (without extensions) for the tax year to which your qualifying contribution(s) will apply. Applications postmarked by this date will be accepted.
